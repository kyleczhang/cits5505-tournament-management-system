# Checkpoint 2: Application Planning

## **Introduction**

In Checkpoint 2 you will demonstrate to your facilitator that you have a concrete idea for your web-application and that you have an initial plan for what functionality your application will provide and what it will look like.

## Preparation for the Checkpoint Meeting

Before the meeting occurs, together as a group you will need to:

- Decide what application you are going to build. Remember it must satisfy the requirements laid out on the `project-description-and-rubrics.md` document.
- Write at least 10 user stories that describe proposed interactions with your system.
- Create a list of the main pages your website will have (e.g. for a movie rating review site: signup page, login page, view movies page, add new movie page, search movie pages etc.)
- Decide which CSS Framework you are going to use.
- (_Optional but strongly encouraged, especially for groups whose meeting is in the later weeks_) Start creating some of your pages with HTML and CSS. The page does not need to be interactive (e.g. no need for buttons to work) but it should be sufficient for a prospective client to understand how the website will function. You should commit all your files to GitHub.

**Note**: do not start implementing the back-end of your website, i.e. the flask server, until after we have covered it in class after the mid-semester break.

## During the Checkpoint Meeting

All members of the team must attend. During the 20 minute meeting:

- You will describe your proposed application to the facilitator.
- You will show them your planning documents and any HTML pages you have made.
- The facilitator will provide feedback.
- The facilitator will ask briefly about your team organisation, e.g. meeting schedule.
